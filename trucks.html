Concrete mixers

Cement mixer trucks are designed to transport and continuously mix concrete while in transit. Their rotating drum prevents the concrete from hardening before delivery. They are essential for construction sites, ensuring fresh and consistent concrete on arrival..

Tractor heads

Powerful towing vehicles designed specifically to pull semi-trailers, featuring a cabin and a, short chassis equipped with a fifth-wheel coupling instead of a cargo bed. They provide the motive power for heavy-duty, long-haul, and specialized cargo transport by distributing trailer weight across their axle(s).

Cargo trucks

Cargo trucks are built to transport large and heavy goods over long distances. They offer high load capacity and durability for commercial and industrial use. These trucks play a vital role in logistics, supply chains, and freight transportation.

Box trucks

A box truck (or cube van) is a commercial vehicle with a chassis-cab design, featuring a separate, enclosed rectangular cargo area. Typically ranging from 10 to 26 feet in length, these trucks are used for local deliveries, furniture moving, and transporting freight, often equipped with rear roll-up doors and liftgates for easy loading.

Dump trucks

Dump trucks are heavy-duty vehicles designed for transporting and unloading loose materials such as sand, gravel, or demolition waste. They feature an open-box bed that can be hydraulically tilted to dump the load, making them essential for construction, mining, and waste management operations.
